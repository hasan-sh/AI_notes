Evaluating verification and validation methods

tech inter


Technological intervention is about targeting certain problems by the means of technology.

THere are different phases(technological intervention processes) for making the intervention systems: - Designing it; define system requirements and target groups > e.g. app to help stop smoking: - epidemiological study, getting data relevant to your idea or the state of arts. - target group: who are those who smoke the most? Then get into that area more(e.g. males/females mainly, elderly ppl or adults ) - requirements: this phase should define what the app will be doing once a person makes an action; and how to react to that.

  • Implementing it: design system architecture and inference engine, code
    • system architecture:
    • inference engine: the logic of the targeted app and how should the app react.
    • programming
  • Testing it: evaluate verification and fine-time parameters; internal validity of the system
    • Do the components work?
    • Does the app(system) give the expected output based on the input?
  • Using it: validation + user evaluation; external validity of the system
    • Does the system help its users achieve their goal?
> cause humans are hard to predict; logic differs most of the time

  • how is the overall UE?
> do the users understand your system and how to use it?


Verification & Validation


verification does the system comply with the rules you've built and relied on?

validation process of checking whether the product meets the users' needs; e.g. did the targeted users actually stopped smoking?

Each app should be presented to two different group: - Control group: ppl who aren't familiar with it and how your system is supposed to work

  • Experimental group: ppl who know how to use it for getting the right results

Research protocol


Important terms to know:

  • Research question: what u want to know.
> is it possible to reduce smoking behavior by using smart app?

  • hypothesis: what the outcome would be
  • experimental and Control condition: ABOVE

  • Independent and dependent variables:
    • condition you applied for your experiment
> smoking variable

  • result that depends on the independent variable
> intervention process

  • subjects/participants: who are they? and are they relevant?
> e.g male, dutch high educated etc..

  • possible confounding variables: things that could modify the results indirectly; and are hard to figure out and should always be considered.
> make sure the results result by you really are expecting, and not by other factors.


Validity: is about figuring out whether the system you've built is doing what it's supposed to.
