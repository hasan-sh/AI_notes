Guest Martin Brikering works at institute for sound and vision(NISV)

> target of audiences(see slides)

NISV is a museum where they present and provide their data. And has extensive archive in storage.

> archives aren't produced by them but collected E.g - radio an tv - documentary film - newsreels - home movies! - web video; - video games;

THey got 18tb. Tapes are stored in locks nad robot arms deliver certain tapes that was requested; old-scholl way no other way?!

Digital services: - public website: - ppl can search through catalogue and find relevant materials. - footage library for pros: they get subscriptions to order them more easily. - educational platform - research platform

Research topics - sustainability: preserving the data and maintain the archive - metadata: describing the collections so that ithey stay accessible - access: how ppl want to consume the data and considering the possible or available provider(product) - digital humanities: to do research on much larger collections of data; for different researchers - users: to know your users.

Challenges: - retrieve fragments from the archive based on queries from the users; - Gather the needed data that helps retrieving fragments. This is done by using other data; metadata

Metadata enrichment: challenging because of the limited resources; since they are public oriented(not commercial)

> Manual annotation was done; which is not efficient.

Annotation: machine analysis used to describe the material and improve the existing ones. - speakers labelling - image recognition; for them it's still in evaluation phases.

A new way of looking at AUdio, video data extraction; hard due to storage size As an alternative they created a game in which people had to watch the game and write a description of what you're seeing in the screen; a reward is points then to get certain collection; e.g. dvd set

OpenIMages's been used by several organizations; wikimedia uses this open api as well!

NISV watned to link wikidata repo into their own data collections to enrich them; useful because wiki data's got a huge set of data. SO, they don't have to collect data that would otherwise have cost a lot of effort; saving resources.

> copyright revents them from collecting data from the right holders, and that's difficult to gain.
