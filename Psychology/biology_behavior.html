Biology and Behavior

Brain structures


  • Cerebellum(little brain): located at the very back of the brain. It is responsible for motor movement and works independently and unconsciously.
> E.g. you can easily think about another matter, while riding your bike. Cerebellum allows you to do so!

  • Subcortical structures: above the brain stem and cerebellum is the forebrain; consists of two cerebral hemispheres. Below the cerebral cortex are the subcortical regions.
    • thalamus: is the gateway to the cortex. All sensory information has to pass through it, except for smell, to the brain. During sleep, thalamus partially shuts down, allowing the brain to rest.
    • hypothalamus: is the master of regulation. It receives inputs from almost everywhere from the body and brain, and then gives its input back. It's indispensible for organism's survival; hunger, thirst and lust. It regulates body temperature
    • hippocampus(sea horse): named because of its shape. Important for forming memories and navigating in our environments.
    • amygdala(almond): named because of its shape. Important for emotions; associating things with emotional responses; protecting animals against danger.
    • basal ganglia: planning and producing movement

Cerebral cortex


The layer of the brain tissue; underlies all important factors: thoughts perception and behavior

  • Corpus callosum; a massive brige that connects the two hemisphere with millions of axons.

Each hemisphere has four lobes: - Occipital: vision; back of the head

  • Parietal: touch; its right part(in the right hemisphere) receives touch information from the left side of the body and its left part receives touch information from the right one. Attention as well. It's in front of the occipital and behind the frontal.
  • Temporal: hearing and memory; since it has the hippocampus and amygdala.
  • Frontal: planning and movement.
    • prefrontal: the front part of the skull, it's important for: attention, working memory, appropriate behavior and personality.

Communication between the Body and Brain


There are two main nervous systems that play important roles in this communication. Central Nervous System and Peripheral Nervous System.

PNS transfers variety of information to the CNS and it also gets responses back and make actions accordingly(e.g. bodily adjustments or behavioral changes). PNS includes two nervous systems:

  • Somatic Nervous System: transmits sensory signals to the CNS through nerves; from the skin, muscles and joints to the spinal cord which gives it to the brain. It then gets instructions whether these parts, skin or joints, should initiate or inhibit actions.
  • Autonomic Nervous System: regulates the internal environment of the body. And has two divisions:
    • Sympathetic: prepares the body for needed actions
    • Parasympathetic: restores the previous rested state of the body.

On top of these two nervous systems, there's another important one => Endocrine Nervous System; which uses hormones for communication. Through its network, the ENS influences behavior, thoughts and actions. ENS is primarily controlled by the hypothalamus

The main difference between the ENS and the other ones, is that ENS is slower, since it uses hormones in transmitting signals.

Gonads: main endocrine's glands involved in sexual behavior; testes in men, ovaries in females

Important Definitions


Plasticity a property in the brain that allows it to change after experience or injury.

Neurogenesis: the production of new neurons; happens in some regions in the brain.

Optogenetics: is a technique to precisely measure when a neuron fires; helps to better understand the association between behavior neuron firing.
