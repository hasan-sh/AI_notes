Watson & Jeopardy

By Chris Welty | Google research


Started 25y ago in sth in the area of AI. Moved to Became a professor at he VU 6y ago. Started at google in 2014.

Now working on google maps; trying to search whatever product user interested in in a semantic manner.

A challenge to AI: natural language(till now; no machine does this perfectly)

> How can we exploit this knowledge to solve problems?

Give: - rich NL questions - over a board domain of knowledge

Deliver: - precise answers: get the most relevant answer to the user's question - accurate confidences: determine likelihood answer is correct - consumable justficaitons: machine should give the source of where it got the answer from - fast response time: too long atm

Problems machines face:

Chris compares human language with chess, that it's a sort of test for some kind of intelligence; moves are countable, language however has an infinite number of possible responses.

Jeopardy: an american game show(quiz show!!) since 1964!

Machine would be good at the broad domain, humans wouldn't. - Understanding the language is difficult for machines, less for humans

  • high Precision is difficult for both
  • Accurate confidence: humans would be confident giving accurate answers, however machines wouldn't
  • High speed, humans would be slower than machines.

> AI was familiar to ppl only through movies, which showed a bad side of it. Watson changed that perspective

The mechanism of answering questions watson uses is: - finding the most relevant answers(texts) that match the question. - Another way is by matching keywrods from the question with the ones in the knowledge base.(like in web search back in the day) - different types of evidence(hybridization): temoral reasoning, statistical paraphrasing and geiospatial reasoning

There are many different layers for finding the answer for a question, watson finds many many answers and keywords, in a layer that's called evidence and hypotheses scoring linear algebra is used to decide which score would most likely be the answer(or keyword of hte answer)
